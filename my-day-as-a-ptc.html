A Day in the Life of an Azure Infrastructure PTC

Starting the Day with Focus

The first few minutes of my workday set the tone for everything. Rather than diving into a crowded inbox, I begin by checking our operational boards. This dashboard reveals my top priorities: new partner consultations, critical follow-ups, performance metrics, and pending operational items. It helps me prepare mentally for the variety of requests—ranging from NetApp tenant migrations to advanced AI integrations—that might appear in my queue.

Reviewing Key Emails & Scheduling

After gaining a high-level view, I review my emails and finalize plans. I see two critical items: a partner needing guidance on migrating NetApp Files from one tenant to another, and a colleague on the Modern Work team wanting input on Azure Copilot scenarios for a partner already proficient with Microsoft 365 Copilot.

First Consultation: NetApp Files Migration

During my consultation, the partner outlines challenges around NetApp Files migration. We discuss best practices, referencing Azure documentation, and confirm prerequisites like network connectivity and identity configurations. By the end, we've clarified the migration plan and uncovered a security enhancement opportunity with Microsoft Sentinel. I note their interest and schedule a follow-up enablement session with our Security PTC.

Wrapping Up

Concluding the day, I feel satisfied. I navigated migrations, expansions, advanced DR discussions, licensing triages, and personal commitments—while honing my expertise. This dynamic, ever-evolving role is exactly why being a Partner Technical Consultant is so rewarding: each challenge fosters growth and helps partners succeed, strengthening our Microsoft community.

Final Thoughts

By day’s end, I see how pivotal our role can be—not only in resolving technical hurdles but in shaping holistic partner solutions. From connecting the right experts to crafting multi-solution roadmaps, each engagement yields tangible outcomes that empower businesses to excel in the cloud. Every day is an adventure, full of breakthroughs, continuous learning, and real-world impact—fueling my passion to do it all again tomorrow.
